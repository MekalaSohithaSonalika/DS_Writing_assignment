Global natural gas production, consumption and market trends in 2024

Global Natural Gas Trends

Natural gas demand grew by 2.5% in 2024, with LNG trade expanding to meet growing demand in Asia and to replace reduced pipeline gas flows in Europe. The fuel maintained its role as a transition fuel in many energy systems.

Global CO2 Emissions Over Time (Fossil Fuels)

Annual CO2 Emissions (Million Tonnes)

Observation: India: Cumulative CO2 Emissions The provided graph illustrates the historical trend of India's greenhouse gas emissions by source from roughly 1850 to 2020, measured in Gigatonnes of CO2 equivalent. It reveals a dramatic overall increase in total emissions, particularly accelerating from the mid-20th century onwards. While Land Use Change CO2 was the dominant emission source in the mid-19th century and saw a significant spike around the 1950s before declining, the profile has fundamentally shifted. By the end of the period, coal combustion clearly dominates, displaying exponential growth since the 1980s to become the largest single source by a significant margin. Methane emissions have also shown a substantial and consistent rise throughout the entire period, establishing themselves as the second-largest contributor. Oil consumption follows as the third-largest source, with steady growth beginning around the 1960s. Nitrous oxide emissions show a slower but steady increase, while contributions from natural gas and CO2 flaring remain comparatively minor throughout the depicted history. Overall, the graph highlights a major transformation towards an emissions profile heavily driven by fossil fuels, especially coal.
